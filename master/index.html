Play it. Learn it.

How can a child play computer games continuously for hours but still have trouble sitting still for five minutes on the school bench? Eduplaytion wants to further develop what makes children and young people become engrossed in video games, by combining play and games with learning and development.

Our vision is to develop short small games that present challenges and focus on problem solving and fun rather than learning. The learning process is a game in itself, and students must quickly understand, memorize, and test abstract rules that have logical consequences. The games are shaped on the basis of game design principles, which makes it as fun to learn math, English and science as playing any video game.

ABOUT NUMETRY

Håvard Tjora

Pedagogue , Teacher, Author.

Håvard Tjora has 16 years of experience as a teacher, with a major in music and mathematics, a master's degree in special education and continuing education in learning styles. He has taught thousands of teachers through hundreds of lectures. He has helped weak students return to school bench and education through the "Blank Sheets" series on TVNorge. And he is the author of four books, including "Mattemagi", which is the best-selling case book for children and youth in recent times. And he has for many years been a columnist in Dagbladet Magasinet.

He is living proof that the teacher who watches, raises and inspires children is not just about credits or research. It is about social interaction, about interaction, about communication and the ability to understand the whole.

Håvard Tjora was awarded the Jonas Prize in 2014 by the Department of Special Education at the University of Oslo. He has "helped create a more spacious and tolerant society," the jury said. Previous prize winners are people like Gunnar Stålsett, Wenche Foss and Johann Olav Koss.

We are lucky to get this Enthusiast working with us in this project and his experience is a good edition to our team.
